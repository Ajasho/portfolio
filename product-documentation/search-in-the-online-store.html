Search in the OSP online store

Learn about the search functionality in your OSP online store and the different ways you can configure it.

OSP includes a suite of features that enable your customers to search for and discover specific products within your online store.

By helping customers find what they’re looking for without having to manually navigate through numerous product categories or listing pages, you can significantly enhance the user experience, increase customer satisfaction, and ultimately drive sales and conversions in your online store.

Key stages and features

When a customer searches for a product in your online store, the following key stages are involved to ensure that the search experience is efficient, accurate, and satisfying:

  1. Entering the search query
  2. Query pre-processing
  3. Matching
  4. Ranking
  5. Displaying search results
  6. Refining results

Entering the search query

The customer begins by entering their search query into the search bar in your online store. This query can be a specific product name, a description, a category, or any relevant keyword.

As customers interact with the search bar, OSP can offer different search suggestions based on popular or previously searched terms. These suggestions help customers avoid typos in the search query and help them pick search terms which display good results, making it easier for them to find products quickly.

For more information about the different search suggestions offered by OSP, see the Search suggestions section in the Online store configuration user guide.

Query pre-processing

The customer’s search query undergoes pre-processing to enhance its accuracy and relevance, and optimise search results.

The major steps involved in this stage are:

  • Search spelling corrections
  • Search synonyms expansion
  • Search redirects

Search spelling corrections

Spelling errors are common and often lead to irrelevant or no search results. If no products are found for the customer’s search term, OSP performs a spelling correction of the search term.

For more information, see the Search spelling corrections explainer.

Search synonyms expansion

This involves expanding the customer’s search query to include synonyms associated with the entered terms. OSP then uses these search synonyms to run additional simultaneous searches and return relevant results even when customers use different terms than what’s used in your product catalog.

You can either configure search synonyms manually or choose to use AI-generated synonyms.

For more information, see the Search synonyms explainer.

Search redirects

OSP also checks for any search redirects associated with the search term that a customer has entered and redirect them to a specific page related to what they are searching for.

For more information on what search redirects are and ways to configure them, see the Search redirects section in the Online store configuration user guide.

Matching

At this stage, OSP compares the pre-processed search query with the database of products in your online store to identify relevant matches. This helps determine the specific products to be included in the search results presented to the customer.

The matching is based on factors such as keywords, product names, attributes, categories, popularity, and relevance scores.

Other factors that impact the products that are matched to a search query are:

  • Product categorization
  • Union tag categories
  • Search antonyms

Product categorization

The way products are organized in your product catalog plays a crucial role in determining the accuracy and relevance of search results matched to a customer’s search query. When consolidating various product types into categories, make sure that the categories don’t contain vastly dissimilar product types to prevent irrelevant products from being returned for a customer’s search query.

Pay extra attention when naming the lowest level child categories. If they contain more than one product type, like ‘Onions & Garlic’, a search for ‘onions’ would also return garlic products.

Union tag categories

A union tag category is a product category which includes multiple product types, and is distinguished by the presence of a conjunction such as ‘and’ or ‘&’ in its name. For example, ‘Vegetarian and vegan foods’, or ‘Scones & clotted cream’.

When a customer searches for ‘vegetarian’, OSP associates it with the ‘Vegetarian and vegan foods’ category. Because this is a union tag category, OSP then looks for any subcategories within it to fetch the most relevant products for the entered search term. Any subcategory containing the search term would be matched while others are excluded from the search results.

So, for the search term ‘vegetarian’, OSP would return products in the ‘Vegetarian & vegan foods > Vegetarian’ subcategory and exclude ones in the ‘Vegetarian & vegan foods > Vegan’ subcategory.

  • You can have different conjunctions configured for use with different languages, for example, for Spanish locales, you can have ‘i’, ‘y’, ‘e’, and ‘&’ in your union tag category names. Speak to your Ocado contact for more information.
  • OSP can only exclude non-matching products within a union tag category if this category includes subcategories for the different product types in its name.
  • The union tag category logic also factors in any search synonyms associated with the entered search term. For example, for a bidirectional search synonym set of ‘zucchini’ and ‘courgette’, and a search term ‘prepared courgette’, OSP will include categories containing either ‘prepared courgette’ or ‘prepared zucchini’.

Search antonyms

Search antonyms are terms similar to the search term that the customer enters but completely differing in meaning, for example, ‘egg’ and ‘egg free’.

When a customer searches for a product in your online store, OSP checks if any antonyms have been assigned to that search term. If there are antonyms assigned, products corresponding to those antonyms are excluded from the search results.

For more information, see the Search antonyms explainer.

Ranking

The search results are ranked based on their relevance to the customer’s search query. Following are the different elements that define how products are ranked on the search results pages:

  • Clustering
  • Merchandise categories
  • Search ranking algorithm

Clustering

Products with shared characteristics or attributes are grouped together into clusters, for example, featured products, favorites. Products can also be grouped based on the categories they are assigned to, for example, product clusters. Clustering helps your customers find the products they are looking for by keeping similar products together.

For more information on the different clusters displayed on your search results pages and how products are sorted within those clusters, see Product display order: Search results pages.

OSP also provides additional options for implementing clustering in your online store, for example, removing product clusters. Speak to your Ocado contact for more information.

Merchandise categories

If a product belongs to more than one category, you can decide which category to use as the primary category the product must be displayed in.

For more information, see the Merchandise categories explainer.

Search ranking algorithm

By default, OSP uses scores from the ranking score algorithm to sort products in the different product clusters. The ranking score algorithm takes into account a combination of the following boosting factors:

  • Search conversion
  • Sales velocity
  • Margin

Search conversion

A product’s search conversion rate is a measure of how often it is ‘added to cart’ after customers search for a specific term, compared to the other products added for the same search term. You can manage the search conversion data in your online store so that your customers see the items they are most likely to add to their cart first.

For more information, see the Search conversion section of the Online store configuration user guide.

Sales velocity

A product’s sales velocity is the number of times it has been purchased over a period of time.

You can use the Online store configuration web app to upload the sales velocity data to rank products by the number of times they’ve been purchased in your online store, and thereby promote products that are more popular to your customers.

For more information, see the Sales velocity section of the Online store configuration user guide.

Margin

A product’s profit margin is the difference between the cost price and the price at which you sell the product to your customers.

You can use the Online store configuration web app to upload the profit margin data and thereby promote products that generate more profit in your online store.

For more information, see the Product profit margin section of the Online store configuration user guide.

Displaying search results

The OSP online store presents a list of products relevant to the customers’ search queries.

Refining results

OSP provides different sorting and filtering options to help customers refine and narrow down their search results.

For information on the different sort options available in your online store, see the Product display order: Search results pages explainer.

For information on the different filtering options available in your online store, see the Product filters explainer.
