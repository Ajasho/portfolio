Product filters

Help your customers find what they are looking for faster using product filters.

Product filters let your customers find products of a specific type on all search results and product category pages. For example, your customers can filter all products that are tagged as suitable for vegetarians, or freezable, or all products of a specific brand.

Product filters help improve the overall customer experience in your online store and help customers easily find products they are looking for.

Filter types

OSP supports the following types of product filters:

  • All promotions
  • New
  • All favourites
  • Brands
  • Dietary and lifestyle

Filters that were effectively applied by the customer are displayed above the filter selection. Customers can also use the Clear all option to remove all previously applied filters.

All promotions filter

The All promotions filter in OSP lets your customers find products that are on promotion and may help to increase the visibility of promoted products in your online store.

All promotions filter - Configuration

When customers apply this filter, OSP only shows products that you have set up a promotion for using the Promotions web app or the Promotions resource.

New filter

The New filter lets your customers find products that you have tagged as new, without having to look through all available products.

This feature encourages customers to explore and discover new products they might not have seen before, thereby enhancing their shopping experience and increasing engagement with your online store.

  • This filter is optional and only enabled on request.
  • The filter only appears if the product listing page has any new products to show.

New filter - Configuration

You can tag a product as ‘new’ by using the Product Propositions resource.

Use the detailedInformation attribute to enter the following name-value pair: new = true.

All favourites filter

The All favourites filter in OSP lets your customers find products that are part of their Favourites. This may help to enhance the visibility of customer’s favourites and increase the number of purchased products on the search results pages.

When customers apply this filter, OSP only shows products that are part of their favourites and relevant to the search term.

Customers can also apply this filter by clicking the Show all favourites card on OSP web, or the Show all favourites button on OSP mobile apps. OSP displays this card and button if the number of customers’ favourite products reaches or exceeds the display limit you set for the Favourites cluster.

This is an optional feature and only enabled on request.

  • The Favourites filter is available on search results pages only.
  • This filter is available to logged-in customers only.
  • This filter is not displayed if the customer doesn’t have any favourite products for the corresponding search term.
  • When this filter is applied, the page only displays the Favourites cluster.

Brands filter

The Brands filter in OSP lets your customers find products of a specific brand or brands they are interested in.

The Brands filter shows a list of up to 100 brands of all products that are displayed on the page the customer is currently viewing. The brands in this list are ordered in descending order of their sales velocity.

The Brands filter is currently not available on the Regulars page.

Brands filter - Configuration

You can define brand names for the Brands filter by using the Product Propositions resource.

Use the detailedInformation attribute to enter the following name-value pair: brand = THE BRAND NAME.

OSP doesn’t consolidate brand names that are named or spelled differently in the Product Propositions resource. For example, if the product listing page contains products configured with different brand name values in the Product Propositions resource - ‘Coca-Cola’ and ‘Coca Cola’, the Brands filter displays them as two separate brands.

  • Apart from rare cases related to regional difference in availability of products, the Brands filter is dynamic. It displays only a list of brands that are actually shown on the page the customer is currently viewing.
  • As OSP doesn’t take into account the product availability per region for this feature, the Brands filter may display a brand of product that is not available in the customer’s region.

Dietary and lifestyle filter

OSP lets you assign special tags to your products, for example, freezable, gluten-free, or suitable for vegetarians to categorize them according to different dietary or lifestyle choices.

When you assign these tags to products, your customers can use these tags to filter products on the product listing pages.

Dietary and lifestyle filters are static. They are displayed as a filter option on a product listing page even if the page does not list any products that are configured with those tags.

Dietary and lifestyle icons

In addition to the static Dietary and lifestyle filters, you can also show the dietary and lifestyle icons displayed on products.

These icons are small images that you can display on product cards and product details pages to categorize products according to a dietary or lifestyle choice. You can use them to highlight the major selling points, or provide any additional information about the product, for example, vegan, gluten-free, or microwavable.

Dietary and lifestyle filters and icons - Configuration

To start using the dietary and lifestyle filters or icons, speak to your Ocado contact to have the tags and icons configured for your online store.

You can then assign the configured tags to the products in your online store yourself by using the Product Propositions resource.
