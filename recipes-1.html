Recipes

Learn how OSP lets you create shoppable recipe content in your online store.

OSP lets you create a shoppable recipe offering by using the Contentful CMS and share them with customers within your online store.

Contentful is a third-party software application that requires additional integration. Speak to your Ocado contact for more information.

Recipes are a great way to connect with your customers, inspire them to try something new, and explore different dishes. This can increase customer engagement and loyalty.

Recipes are also a great way to improve traffic to your online store and acquire new customers. This feature lets your customers purchase products required to complete the recipes directly in your online store.

Recipe home page

The recipe home page is the main space for displaying recipes, recipe collections, and recipe collection groups.

You can configure your recipe home page using the following elements:

  • Static banner image - the main image for your recipe home page.
  • Recipe group - a group of recipes that you want to present together.
  • Collection group - a group of recipe collections.
  • Expanded collection - a space to highlight a specific recipe collection and promote some recipes that belong to that collection.
  • Saved recipes - a space to display customers’ saved recipes on the recipe home page.

For detailed instructions on how to configure the different elements on the recipe home page, see the Recipe home page section in Online store contentful via Contentful user guide.

Search

The recipe home page includes a search feature so the customers can find recipes more easily. OSP matches the search terms that the customer enters with the recipe title, recipe description, or the recipe search terms to display the relevant recipes.

All Collections page

The All Collections page lists all the recipe collections that you have configured in OSP in alphabetical order (A-Z).

Customers can access this page in the following ways:

  • When a search for a recipe on the recipe home page does not return any results, OSP displays a no results message and Browse button. Customers can click this button to go to the All Collections page.

  • Customers can also click the Browse collections card at the bottom of the recipe home page to go to the All Collections page.

The All Collections page makes it easier for search engines web crawlers to access all the recipe content available in your OSP.

The All Collections page only displays recipe collections that are available in the customer’s region and language.

Saved recipes

The recipe home page includes a Saved recipes section where customers can see and scroll through their saved recipes. By clicking a specific recipe, they can go to the respective Recipe details page.

You can rearrange the position of the Saved recipes section, depending on where you want customers’ saved recipes to appear on your recipe home page. See recipe home page for more information.

The Saved recipes section only displays up to 10 of the most recent recipes saved by the customer. To see all recipes, customers can click See all to go to the Saved recipes page.

The Saved recipes page lists all the recipes that a customer has saved, ordered by the most recently saved recipes first.

  • Customers can only access the Saved recipes page by clicking See all in the Saved recipes section on the recipe home page.
  • The Saved recipes section and the Saved recipes page are displayed only if customers have at least one saved recipe.

Customer journey - shoppable ingredients

The recipe feature converts ingredients in the recipes into products that customers can browse and purchase.

When customers browse recipes and decide they want to buy the ingredients, they can do so by using the Shop for ingredients button.

Customers are then presented with a list of all ingredients in the recipe and a set of search results for products corresponding to each of those ingredients. Customers can buy the required products to complete the recipe.

You can configure the number of search results that display per ingredient. Speak to your Ocado contact for more information.

By default, the headings of the ingredient sections on the Shop for ingredients page are displayed as ‘Search results for + {Ingredient name}’. You can modify the text in these headings by using Lokalise.

Customer journey - save and share recipes

Customers can save a recipe they like or wish to visit later by clicking Save recipe on the Recipe details page.

Customers can find a recipe they saved:

  • in the Saved recipes section on the recipe home page, if the recipe is among the 10 most recent recipes saved by the customer.
  • on the Saved recipes page by clicking See all in the Saved recipes section.

Customers can also share a recipe by clicking Share recipe on the Recipe details page.

The Share recipe functionality is only available for the mobile app version of your online store.

Customers can click Recipe saved on the Recipe details page to unsave a recipe.

Display recipes on your store home page

You can promote your recipe content and link to specific pages in your recipe offering by using store home page cards. These cards appear in a separate section called Recipes on your store’s home page. Customers can click a card and navigate to a specific page within your recipe offering. You can display a maximum of 6 cards on the store’s home page.

You can link the store home page card to either of the following pages:

  • Recipe home page
  • Recipe details
  • Recipe collection

See Store home page card for information on how to create and configure store home page cards.

Promote recipes on search results and product category pages

You can enhance the visibility of the existing recipes and recipe collections within your online store by displaying them as recipe content cards on your search results and product category pages.

You can create these recipe content cards and link them to specific search terms or product categories. When customers use these search terms or browse these product categories to find a product, OSP displays these cards among the product cards on the corresponding search results or product category pages.

You can configure the exact placement of these cards on the respective pages. You can also display mutiple recipe content cards on the same page.

Customers can click a card and navigate to a specific recipe or recipe collection within your recipe offering. Depending on the target page they are are linked to, the cards are displayed as follows:

  • Recipe content card showing an image and details of the linked recipe.

  • Recipe content card showing the main image and the images of the first four recipes within the linked recipe collection.

See Recipe content card for information on how to create and configure recipe content cards for your search results and product categories pages.

Localization

If your online store supports multiple languages, you can display your recipe offering in all those languages.

However, you must provide all customer-facing recipe information in each of those languages.

For example, if your online store is available in English and French, you must supply all customer-facing recipe information in both these languages.

SEO settings

You can define and configure SEO settings for the different pages in your recipe offering so that the pages are more findable by search engines.

The recipe offering supports the following SEO features:

  • Google recipe schema - pages in your recipe offering are structured according to the Google recipe schema that lets search engines like Google better understand the content on the pages. This structure also lets Google display the content in more engaging ways that draw attention to the recipe pages increasing the traffic to your online store.

  • Tagging - you can use different tags to provide additional data about your recipe pages, making them easier to find for search engines and website visitors. The recipe feature supports the following tags on all the recipe pages:

    • H1 tags
    • Title tags
    • Description tags (also called Meta tags)

    See SEO settings for information on how to configure tags for the different types of pages in your recipe offering.

  • Human-readable URLs - you can configure human-readable URLs that make your recipe pages easier to find and rank higher in search engine results. Auto-generated URLs that include number and character strings often confuse search engines and site visitors. You can define specific keywords and phrases related to the content on the page in the URL slug for the respective pages. This makes it easier for website visitors and search engines to find the pages.

Onboarding and bulk-uploading of recipes

The recipe feature is optional, and is built and configured using the Contentful platform. Speak to your Ocado contact to set up a Contentful account and enable the recipe feature for you.

If you have any pre-existing recipe content from other sources, you can have them validated and bulk-uploaded to OSP before the recipe feature is enabled in your online store. Speak to your Ocado contact for more information.

How to use the Recipes feature

For detailed instructions on how to configure this feature and create different content types for your recipe offering in Contentful, see the Recipes user guide.

Analytics

To measure different customer interactions or occurrences within your recipes offering, new events are available in the OSP data layer. These events are based on the Google Analytics 4 format and are available for both the web and mobile apps versions of your online store.

For detailed information on these events, see Recipe events.

These events only appear in the data layer if you have integrated with Contentful and have the Recipes feature enabled in your OSP.
